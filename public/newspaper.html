Debut Author Spotlight

First novels worth your next road trip detour
Debut Novel
The Familiar
Leigh Bardugo

Bardugo steps into adult literary fiction with a story set in Inquisition-era Spain. Luzia Cotado, a scullery maid who hides small magical abilities, is thrust into the service of a nobleman who wants to use her gifts to advance his standing at court.

Historical Fiction Magic Realism Spain

Debut Novel
I Need You to Read This
Jessa Maxwell

A literary thriller about a book editor who receives a manuscript that seems to predict real murders in her building. Told with sharp wit and a mounting dread that keeps you flipping pages long past midnight. Maxwell is an immediate name to watch.

Pure Colour
Sheila Heti

Heti's lyrical meditation on grief, beauty, and what it means to mourn a parent — and then a whole world. Formally experimental, emotionally devastating, and unexpectedly funny in places. A short book that takes weeks to leave you.

Curated literary routes from the community
This Month's Featured Route
The Pacific Northwest Literary Loop
Oregon · Washington · Northern California  ·  7 days
Start at Powell's Books in Portland — the world's largest independent bookstore — and wind north through the mist-soaked forests that inspired Le Guin, Kesey, and Raymond Carver. Cross into Washington for the Olympic Peninsula, then south along the California redwoods through Eureka to Arcata's independent scene.
"Powell's alone justified the entire trip. We stayed three hours."

Delta Literary Tour
Mississippi  ·  4 days  ·  Submitted by @readingandrolling

From Oxford (home of Square Books and Faulkner's Rowan Oak) south through Greenwood and Greenville. Faulkner, Eudora Welty, Richard Wright — the literary geography of the Delta is denser than anywhere in America.

Flannery O'Connor Country
Georgia · Tennessee  ·  5 days  ·  Submitted by @boooksandroads

Savannah to Milledgeville (Andalusia farm, O'Connor's home) up through Atlanta and into the Tennessee foothills. Gothic, strange, deeply American. Don't miss the Flannery O'Connor Childhood Home in Savannah.
